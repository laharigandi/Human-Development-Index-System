About HDI

What is the Human Development Index?

The HDI is a composite statistic developed by the UNDP to measure and rank countries by levels of human development across three key dimensions.

Health Dimension

Measured by life expectancy at birth, reflecting the ability to live a long and healthy life.

Range 20 – 90 years
Education Dimension

Measured by mean years of schooling and expected years of schooling for children.

Mean Schooling 0 – 20 years
Income Dimension

Measured by Gross National Income per capita (PPP $), reflecting the standard of living.

GNI Range $100 – $150,000
Classification

HDI Categories

Countries are classified into four development tiers based on their composite HDI score.

Very High

HDI ≥ 0.800

Advanced economies with excellent health, education, and income standards.

Norway, Switzerland, Germany
High

0.700 – 0.799

Developing nations with good progress in human development indicators.

Brazil, China, Malaysia
Medium

0.550 – 0.699

Countries with moderate development facing challenges in key indicators.

India, Kenya, Bangladesh
Low

HDI < 0.550

Nations facing significant development challenges requiring urgent attention.

Niger, Mali, Chad
